Cheap Print-and-Play (PnP) Netrunner

[Page last updated 05/07/2025]

You may have heard about Null Signal Games (NSG), the non-profit that rebooted Android Netrunner after Fantasy Flight Games (FFG) stopped printing new sets. Null Signal Games releases all of their card art free for people to print their own copies. This document contains helpful links and a price breakdown for how much time and money it would cost for you to make your own sets!

The Cost if You Don't Make Your Own Cards

As of May 2025, each of the eight Null Signal sets are available for purchase for between $45 and $60+ each depending on where you buy them.

  • Drive Through Cards
  • Make Playing Cards
  • Null Signal Games' own store

If you factor in the cost of tokens and a card-holder box(es), this means the total cost is somewhere between $410 and $530. Yikes!

The Cost if You Make Your Own Cards

The Punchline

Total Cost (rounded up): Printing ($60) + Paper Cutter ($40) + Card Sleeves ($37) + Tokens ($21) + Storage ($20) ~= $180

Printing

The cheapest method I've found was to write a simple, hacky python script which extracts the card images from the official print-and-play PDF's and puts them on images in the right dimension for printing as photos. The current eight sets come out to a grand total of 780 photos.

Printing Options:

  • Snapfish -- This is the option I recommend, particularly when they have their "1-cent" (so-called) photo deals, which seem to run about once every month. They charge a lot on shipping, but you still get a good price per photo. I was able to order the whole collection of photos for under $60 -- under 7.7 cents per photo.
  • Walmart Photo -- Walmart has a consistent price of 14 cents per photo, yielding a total order price of $110.
  • [any photo deal you can find]

The Actual Photos:

  • System Gateway
  • Elevation
  • Ashes Cycle
  • Borealis Cycle
  • Liberation Cycle

These photos are intended to be printed as 4x6 prints. Note that they are specifically designed for Snapfish, which auto-crops part of the image when they print it. After printing the cards come out to the standard 3.5x2.5" size.

Card-Cutting

You will want a guillotine paper-cutter like one of the following:

  • Swingline Guillotine Trimmer ($40)
  • "Amazon Basics" Guillotine Trimmer ($30)

Sleeving

Photos are way too flimsy and see-through to use as cards without some kind of backing or card sleeve. Sleeving provides a nice looking card-back and some much-needed structural support.

The cheapest sleeves I found: "Kitoyz" Red and Blue 500-packs of matte card sleeves for $14 each. The red pack will handle all your runner cards and the blue pack all of your corp cards.

You'll also want a few clear card sleeves for the flippable identities.

Time You're Busy Cutting and Sleeving

When I get into a focused groove, I can knock out a set in about 1 hour. This would bring the total amount of work to 8 hours, but that's quite optimistic. Realistically you're probably looking at 12+ hours of work, especially if you get distracted reading the cards.

Tokens

You can get nice, cheap Netrunner tokens from Burger Tokens -- $20 plus $1 in pennies.

Storage

I made my own storage with a cardboard box and some sheet metal, but you can buy boxes for this sort of thing.
