Cheap Print-and-Play (PnP) Netrunner

[Page last updated 07/13/2025]

You may have heard about Null Signal Games (NSG), the non-profit that rebooted Android Netrunner after Fantasy Flight Games (FFG) stopped printing new sets. Null Signal Games releases all of their card art free for people to print their own copies. This document contains helpful links and a price breakdown for how much time and money it would cost for you to make your own sets!

The Cost if You Don't Make Your Own Cards

As of May 2025, each of the eight Null Signal sets are available for purchase for between $45 and $60+ each depending on where you buy them.

  • Drive Through Cards
  • Make Playing Cards
  • Null Signal Games' own store

If you factor in the cost of tokens and a card-holder box(es), this means the total cost is somewhere between $410 and $530. Yikes!

The Cost if You Make Your Own Cards

The Punchline

Total Cost: Printing ($60) + Paper Cutter ($30) + Tokens ($21)
+ Sleeving and Storage ($28 to $77 [see options below]) ~= $139 to $188

Printing

The cheapest method I've found was to write some simple, hacky python scripts which extract the card images from the official print-and-play PDF's and constructs images with the right dimensions for printing as photos. The current eight sets come out to a grand total of 780 photos.

You can download the photos at these links:

  • System Gateway
  • Elevation
  • Ashes Cycle
  • Borealis Cycle
  • Liberation Cycle

These photos are intended to be printed as 4x6 prints. Note that they are specifically designed for Snapfish, which auto-crops part of the image when they print it. After printing the cards come out to the standard 3.5x2.5" size.

Printing Options:

  • Snapfish -- This is the option I recommend, particularly when they have their unlimited "1-cent" (so-called) photo deals, which seem to run about once every month. They charge a lot on shipping, but you still get a good price per photo. I was able to order the whole collection of photos for under $60 -- under 7.7 cents per photo.
  • Walmart Photo -- Walmart has a consistent price of 14 cents per photo, yielding a total order price of $110.
  • [any photo deal you can find]

  • Jisesili Guillotine Trimmer ($30)

Tokens

You can get nice, cheap Netrunner tokens from Burger Tokens -- $20 plus 50 cents in pennies.

Sleeving and Storage

Photos are way too flimsy and see-through to use as cards without some kind of backing or card sleeve. Sleeving provides a nice looking card-back and some much-needed structural support.

For reference, there are roughly 840 corp cards in the current collection and roughly 720 runner cards.

Option A ($28 - $31)

Sleeve only your drafted decks in top-quality card sleeves: 100 matte "Dragon Shield" sleeves for $12-$15.

Store your sleeved and your unsleeved cards in a box like this ($16). You may wish to make some dividers of your own out of an old cardboard box.

Option B ($70+)

Sleeve all the cards in some of the cheapest sleeves to be found: "Kitoyz" Red and Blue 500-packs of matte card sleeves for $14 each. Get two packs of each color (blue for corp, red for runner) or three packs of the same color plus a 100-pack for monetary efficiency. My apologies that they are from Amazon.

Store them in a box like in option A.

For the flippable identities, you can either acquire some clear card sleeves or you can just tape two card sleeves back-to-back.

If you feel that these card sleeves are too flimsy or the backs are too see-through for play, you can buy a regular deck of cards and insert those into the sleeves of your drafted deck since a typical drafted deck is under 50 cards. Alternatively you could just have nice sleeves for your drafted decks, like in Option A.

Option C ($49 - $77)

Sleeve your drafted cards like in Option A and store them in a box like this ($11).

Then store your unsleeved cards in two 2" D-ring binders (ranging from $8 to $17 each) with card sleeves for binders like these ($10 for 900 sleeves, $17 for 1800 sleeves). Note that I have not tested these sleeve pages, so I cannot vouch for their durability. I also do not know if you'd need to sleeve the photos individually or if you can safely stuff the 3 identical photos per distinct netrunner card into a single sleeve.

Time You're Busy Cutting and Sleeving

When I get into a focused groove, I can knock out a set in about 1 hour. This would bring the total amount of work to 8 hours, but that's quite optimistic. Realistically you're probably looking at 12+ hours of work at least, especially if you get distracted reading the cards.
